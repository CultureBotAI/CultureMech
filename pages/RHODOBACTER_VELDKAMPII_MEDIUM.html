Preparation Protocol

  1. MIX
    supplement medium 28 with 0,25% Yeast-extract, 0,3% Na-acetate, 2mg/l Vitamin B12 and 10ml/l Standard vitamin solution (see below).
  2. FILTER_STERILIZE
    Adjust the pH with filter-sterilised 1M Na2CO3 to 7.1-7.3.
  3. ALIQUOT
    If needed, aliquot into sterile, N<sub>2</sub> gassed screw-cap tubes under N<sub>2</sub> gas.
  4. MIX
    During the first 24 h, the iron of the medium precipitates in the form of black flocks. No other sediment should arise in the otherwise clear medium.
  5. AUTOCLAVE
    Aliquot Solution A into 100 mL screw-cap bottles, filled with 46 mL each. Bubble with N2/CO2 and autoclave at 121°C for 15 min (as decribed below).
  6. MIX
    Prepare the following solutions (resazurin, bicarbonate and Pfennig's heterotrophic salts) and sterilize as given below.
  7. COOL
    Add bicarbonate solution and Pfennig's heterotrophic salts to the resazurin (complete volumina, i.e. 50 mL bicarbonate solution and 26 mL Pfennig's heterotrophic salts solution). Bubble with CO2 in an ice bath under sterile conditions.
  8. MIX
    Fill 50 ml of this mixture to each bottle of solution A (46 mL + 50 mL).
  9. MIX
    Before use, add 4 ml sulfide solution (1.5%) and 0.1 ml Vitamin B12 solution to each 100 mL bottle.
  10. MIX
    Feed the actively growing culture periodically with neutralized 3% solution of sodium sulfide (use 1 -3 mL/100 mL depending on strain and cultivation stage) to replenish sulfide and with other supplement solutions (see Ref. 3365).
  11. AUTOCLAVE
    Aliquot Solution A into 100 mL screw-cap bottles, filled with 46 mL each. Bubble with N2/CO2 and autoclave at 121°C for 15 min.
  12. AUTOCLAVE
    Prepare in a screw-cap bottle, bubble with N2 to replace air, close tightly and autoclave.
  13. FILTER_STERILIZE
    Bubble with CO2 and filter sterilize into sterile, gas-tight, 100 ml screw-cap bottle.
  14. AUTOCLAVE
    Autoclave in a cotton-stoppered Erlenmeyer flask with an outlet tube for medium, connected to a glass outlet at the bottom of the vessel and has, at the other end, a silicon rubber tube with a pinch cock and a bell for aseptic dispensing of the medium into bottles.
  15. COOL
    Cool to room temperature under an atmosphere of N2/CO2 in an ice bath.
  16. FILTER_STERILIZE
    Filter sterilize into sterile, gas-tight, 100 ml screw-cap bottle.
  17. FILTER_STERILIZE
    Filter sterilized
  18. AUTOCLAVE
    The sulfide solution is prepared in a 250 ml screw-capped bottle with a butyl rubber septum and a magnetic stirrer. The solution is bubbled with nitrogen gas, closed and autoclaved for 15 min. at 121°C. After cooling to room temperature the pH is adjusted to about 7.0 by adding of sterile 2 M H2SO4 drop-wise with a syringe without opening the bottle.
